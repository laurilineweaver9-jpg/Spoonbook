The Escalating Existential Scale

Honest self-assessment is the whole point. Use your spoons wisely. 🥄

1
🌈 Utopia
I could conquer the world today.
▾
What This Means
  • This almost never happens — honor it completely
  • Your body is giving you a gift today
Do This
  • Do the thing you've been wanting to do — adventure, spontaneity, a project, a plan
  • Go outside, move your body in a way that feels joyful
  • Connect with someone you love
  • Don't waste it on errands or obligation
Gentle Reminder
  • Pace yourself — a 1 today doesn't have to mean a 7 tomorrow
  • Hydrate. Eat well. Sleep on time.
2
✨ Sparkly
Feeling surprisingly human.
▾
What This Means
  • Better than your baseline — energy is present, body is cooperating
  • A genuinely good day worth leaning into
Do This
  • Take yourself out — somewhere you enjoy, not just somewhere useful
  • Start a project or pick up one you've been avoiding
  • Move your body — Pilates, a walk, yoga, Peloton
  • Connect socially if it feels good
Therapies That Fit
  • Physical activity · Journaling · Time outside · Meditation
3
😊 Doing Okay
I'll take it.
▾
What This Means
  • Functional and relatively comfortable — this is a good day by your standards
  • Symptoms are present but manageable
Do This
  • Normal activities are fair game — listen to what sounds appealing
  • Stretching or gentle movement is a good investment
  • Nourish well: real meals, hydration, your supplements
  • Enjoy something small — tea, a book, a show you love
Therapies That Fit
  • Stretching · Yoga · Bath · Aromatherapy · Journaling
4
😐 Meh
Functional-ish.
▾
What This Means
  • Getting through it — not thriving, not crashing
  • Pain and fatigue are present but not dominant
  • You can probably do things, just not enthusiastically
Do This
  • Keep commitments that are low-effort; give yourself permission to drop the rest
  • Prioritize comfort: warm blanket, favorite tea, easy food
  • Gentle movement if the body is willing — don't force it
  • Early bedtime is a valid choice — don't fight the need for rest
Therapies That Fit
  • Heating pad or ice · Compression · Calm music · Comfort TV
Watch
  • If Meh becomes a pattern for several days, it may signal a building flare
5
☕ Running on Caffeine & Hope
Pushing through on fumes.
▾
What This Means
  • You're managing but it's costing you — energy is borrowed, not earned
  • Pain and fatigue are significant; concentration may be difficult
Do This
  • Reduce obligations — cancel or postpone anything non-essential
  • Don't rely on caffeine to mask the need for rest
  • Hydrate and eat even if appetite is low
  • Check in with your body: what specifically hurts? Where?
  • Consider PRN medications if pain is spiking
Therapies That Fit
  • Rest · Heating pad · Weighted blanket · Guided meditation · Warm shower
Watch
  • This level often precedes a flare — pay attention to trends
  • Check SpO₂ if breathlessness increases
6
🫠 Questionable Choices
The day is making decisions for me.
▾
What This Means
  • Significantly unwell — symptoms are loud and in charge
  • Cognitive fog, pain, and fatigue are all competing for attention
  • Normal tasks feel unreasonable
Do This
  • Cancel plans without guilt — this is not optional rest, it's necessary
  • Take your PRN meds: Tramadol for pain, Zofran for nausea, Lasix if swelling
  • Soft food, warm liquids, small amounts
  • Don't push through — resting now costs less than crashing later
Environment
  • Dim lights · Quiet space · Comfortable temperature · Weighted blanket
Therapies That Fit
  • TENS unit · Epsom salt bath · Progressive muscle relaxation · White noise · Heat or ice
Watch
  • Monitor SpO₂ — get on O₂ if dropping
  • Note if this is a new flare pattern or worsening of existing symptoms
7
🌧️ The Wheels Are Wobbling
Holding it together but barely.
▾
What This Means
  • Active flare territory — body is in protest mode
  • Pain is severe, fatigue is profound, multiple systems may be involved
  • This is real. It is not an overreaction.
Do This
  • Full rest — bed or couch only
  • O₂ on if SpO₂ is dropping or breathlessness increases
  • PRN medications: Tramadol, Zofran, Lasix + Potassium if swelling
  • Bland food only: broth, crackers, toast
  • Small frequent sips of water
  • Ask for help — this is not the day to do it alone
Environment
  • Quiet room · Soft lighting · Light touch only — no pressure on inflamed areas
Therapies That Fit
  • Heating pad or ice (ask what helps) · Neck massager · Magnesium lotion · Box breathing
Watch
  • Check SpO₂ every few hours
  • Monitor for signs of infection: fever, spreading redness, unusual pain
  • If symptoms worsen toward an 8 or 9 — consider contacting your care team
8
🚨 Dumpster Fire
Everything is on fire and I am in the fire.
▾
What This Means
  • Severe flare — body is in crisis mode
  • Multiple systems may be involved simultaneously
  • You should not be alone right now
Do This
  • Contact someone — your husband, a friend, your care team
  • O₂ on immediately if any breathlessness or SpO₂ concern
  • All PRN medications as appropriate
  • Nothing by mouth except water and bland food if tolerated
  • Lie down in your most comfortable position
  • Consider calling your rheumatologist or care team today
Environment
  • Full quiet · No obligations · Absolute minimal stimulation
ER Warning Signs at This Level
  • SpO₂ below 88% not responding to O₂
  • HR in 50s or BP in 80s
  • Throat swelling or difficulty swallowing
  • Fever over 103°F
  • Chest pain with shortness of breath
9
💀 Absolute Clusterfuck
My body has fully betrayed me.
▾
What This Means
  • You are in serious distress — this is a medical situation
  • Someone else needs to be making decisions right now
Do This — Right Now
  • Call someone immediately — you should not be managing this alone
  • O₂ on now, no question
  • Lie still, stay warm, do not exert yourself
  • Strongly consider going to the ER or calling your care team urgently
Tell the ER
  • Relapsing Polychondritis + Seronegative RA — systemic autoimmune
  • Immunosuppressed: Cellcept, Prednisone, Cimzia
  • Allergies: Ancef, Vancomycin, Compazine, Adhesive Tape, CHG
  • Home O₂ patient, tunneled port, bilateral hip replacements, cervical disc prosthetics
  • History of sepsis (2017) and anaphylactoid reaction to anesthesia
10
🖕 Fuck
This is a medical emergency.
▾
Stop. Call 911 or go to the ER now.
  • You have reached the limit of what can be managed at home
  • Do not wait. Do not minimize. Do not drive yourself.
Call 911 If Any of These Are Present
  • SpO₂ below 88% not improving on oxygen
  • HR in 50s or BP in 80s — call 911
  • Chest pain with shortness of breath
  • Confusion, inability to speak or communicate clearly
  • Throat swelling or severe difficulty swallowing
  • Loss of consciousness or near-fainting
  • Fever over 103°F with low BP or confusion
Tell Every Provider — Every Time
  • 🚫 ALLERGIES: Ancef · Vancomycin · Compazine · Adhesive Tape · CHG
  • Immunosuppressed: Cellcept + Prednisone + Cimzia
  • Tunneled port · Home O₂ · Bilateral hip replacements · Cervical disc prosthetics C5–C7
  • Severe anesthesia reaction: blistering rash — flag before any procedure
  • Relapsing Polychondritis + Seronegative RA
You Are Not Overreacting
  • A 10 is real. You have a complex, serious medical history.
  • You know your body. Trust it.
